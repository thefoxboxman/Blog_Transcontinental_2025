Kalgoorlie

Today we turn north toward Kalgoorlie, 400km inland from Esperance. Norseman is the only town along the route and it is exactly halfway.

Norseman is the western end of the fabled "Nullabor" route. All the traffic passing between the east and west coasts of Australia pass through Norseman.

But first we stop at the small hamlet of Grass Patch for breakfast.Despite the lack of people it is a very attractive stop. A lot of effort has been put into the towns presentation.

North of Grass Patch near the town of Salmon Gum we enter the massive Great Western Woodland...

The Great Western Woodlands is located in the southwest of Australia. The woodlands cover almost 16,000,000 hectares (40,000,000 acres), a region larger in size than England and Wales. The boundary of the Great Western Woodlands runs from the Nullarbor Plain in the east to the Western Australian Wheatbelt in the west; from north of Esperance through to the inland mulga country and deserts that are found north of Kalgoorlie
