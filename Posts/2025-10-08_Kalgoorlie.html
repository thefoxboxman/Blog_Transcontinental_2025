Kalgoorlie

Today we turn north toward Kalgoorlie, 400km inland from Esperance. Norseman is the only town along the route and it is exactly halfway.

The Road to Kalgoorlie

Norseman is the western end of the fabled "Nullabor" route. All the traffic passing between the east and west coasts of Australia pass through Norseman.

But first we stop at the small hamlet of Grass Patch for breakfast.Despite the lack of people it is a very attractive stop. A lot of effort has been put into the towns presentation.

North of Grass Patch near the town of Salmon Gum we enter the massive Great Western Woodland...

The Great Western Woodlands is located in the southwest of Australia. The woodlands cover almost 16,000,000 hectares (40,000,000 acres), a region larger in size than England and Wales. The boundary of the Great Western Woodlands runs from the Nullarbor Plain in the east to the Western Australian Wheatbelt in the west; from north of Esperance through to the inland mulga country and deserts that are found north of Kalgoorlie

Norseman has quite a display of street art too...

We took our time to stop and smell the flowers along the way...

Coolgardie

Once the third largest city in Western Austalia. Discovery of gold at Coolgardie in 1892 established the eastern gold fields.

Coolgardie is now a sleepy little village with some grand old buildings...

Kalgoorlie...the main event

Kalgoorlie was established in 1893 and would soon overshaddow Coolgardie in size and gold production. Unlike Coolgardie, Kalgoorlie's gold mining unabated.

Kalgoorlie as you would imagine has some grand old architecture.

Despite the grand buildings, many of Kalgoorlie's later buildings are sadly neglected and vacant.

Mining continues of the very edge of the city. In underground mines...

And in the Super Pit. Once Australia's largest open cut gold mine. It produces 15t to 20t of gold per year...

Hammond Park is an oasis in the dry dusty landscape...

Boulder...

Boulder is Kalgoorlie's little sister. Once it's own seperate city it is now a suburb of the larger Kalgoorlie-Boulder city.

Lake Lefroy...

About 50km south of Kalgoorlie is a small mining town of Kambalda on the shore of Lake LeFroy; a mostly dry salt lake. This made for some impressive drone photography.
