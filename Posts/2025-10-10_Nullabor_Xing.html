Nullabor Crossing

Today and for the next few days we are headed east across the "Nullabor". From Norseman to Ceduna. A 1201km drive with nothing but roadhouses punctuating the wildneress.

The road is wide, flat and straigt. One 146km (90 mile) section is the longest straight section of road in Australia

For two days we drive through the Great Western Woodland. Now that's a forest.

We plan to do the drive in 3 days. Our first overnight stop is at the Cocklebiddy Roadhouse.

The next morning we go down our first hill as we come down the escarpment at Madura. The escarpement runs the entire length across the Great Australian Bight. At Madura the highway goes down on to the coastal plain.

Across the Nullabor drive a hole in the ground has to pass as a tourist attraction...

The sign tells the story...

Occasionally the far remote areas a section of road is a designated Royal Flying Doctor airstrip.

Not a pedestrian crossing for emus but the start/end of the emergency airstrip.

Later in the day we go back up the escarpement at Eucla. Eucla is about 20km west of the Western Australia - South Australia border.

After nearly two days drivimng we get our first glimpe of the Great Australian Bight and the cliffs.

Now in South Australia and we are travelling beside the magnificant cliffs of the Great Australian Bight.

Every 20 or 30km there is a lookout point. This is where the drone earns it's money...

Futher east into South Australia we finally get to the real Nullabor...

Hundreds of kilometers with tree flat plains that stretch to the horizon. And this is just the south eastern corner of the Nullabor.

Tonight we are camping at the Nullabor Roadhouse...

Which is a long way from anywhere...

Sunset on the Nullabor...

The next day we arrive into Ceduna. This is as far as we travelled earlier in the year when we toured around South Australia. At this point we have completed our circumnavigation of Australia...

The next morning it was very foggy leaving Ceduna...

And the spider webs were begging to be photographed...
