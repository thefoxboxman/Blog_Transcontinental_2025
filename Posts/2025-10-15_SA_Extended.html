South Australia - Extended

Now it is time to head for home. Less sightseeing and more distance travelled. But first there are a few spots in South Australia we want to check out along the way. We take a detour to the Adelaide seaside suburb of Glenelg to see some newly install glass sculptures...

Glenelg...

From Glenelg we head north across the rolling hills of the Barossa Valley towards Kupunda. The winter rainfall has not been enough to erase the drought and the crops are struggling to finish. May growers are deciding to cut their wheat crops for hay.

There are some late crops of Canola still managing to flower...

On the way to Kupunda we take a detour to photograph again the "Midnight Oil House" near Burra. This time instead of bring surrounded by barren fields there is a flowering crop of Canola.

Kupunda...

Kupunda is a super cute only copper mining town with a strong Cornish histroy. Kupunda was also the home base for Sir Sidney Kidman and the empire he built.

The outstanding old buildings are testament to the wealth of the copper mining operation...

We are always impress with a town with great street art...

The also famous Sir Sidney Kidman has a memorial in the main street of Kupunda...

The old copper mine in on the edge of town.

The mine histroy is well preserved and presented

Kupunda is in the process of having it's silos painted to reflect it's histroy. It is interesting to see how it is done.

Lake Bonney

Our last stop in South Australia is at Lake Bonney for some sunset and sunrise photo opportunities...

Across South Australia and into Victoria and on into New South Wales we follow the Murray River.

The Riverina

From here we turn north following along the Murrumbidgee River across the Hay Plains...

Our next overnight stop is at Junee in the Riverina region. Here the season has been much better and the crops are showing it...

And a herd of "fat as mud" beef cattle being moved "old style" along the road.

Yass

From Junee it is onto to have a look at the city of Yass.

...

From here it is onto Sydney for a few days, then 3 days driving north up coast to home.
